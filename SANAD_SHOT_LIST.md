## DIRECTOR'S NOTES

### What this film is NOT saying
- SANAD cures disease.  
- Technology replaces doctors.  
- The old system always fails.  

### What this film IS saying
- The system already works — SANAD makes it work *together*.  
- The difference between SANAD and no-SANAD is measured in hours, complications, and SAR — not life vs. death.  
- The father survives in both worlds. **The question is what that survival costs.**

### The line that carries everything
> *اليوم تُم إنقاذ مريض. وغدًا يمكن إنقاذ ملايين.*  
> "Today, one patient was saved. Tomorrow, millions can be."

This is not hyperbole. It is the natural extension of demonstrating the system at scale.  
It earns the investor's imagination without manipulating their skepticism.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| VO talent | Male, 40s, measured cadence — not newsreader, not dramatic |
| Music | Original score preferred. Reference: Hans Zimmer "Time" (Inception) — the patience, not the climax |
| Color grade | Desaturated, tungsten-warm in Act 1 (without SANAD). Cooler, cleaner, higher contrast in Act 2 (with SANAD). Same locations — different world |
| Title cards | White on pure black only. No motion graphics. The words carry themselves |
| Shot 18 (the hug) | No close-up on faces. Medium shot. Let the viewer project. |
| Network visualization (Shot 16) | 2D line-drawing aesthetic, not 3D render. Credibility over spectacle |
