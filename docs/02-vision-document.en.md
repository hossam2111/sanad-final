# SANAD — Vision Document

**From Decision Support to a National Health-Intelligence Fabric**

*Behind Every Decision*

## Mission

To build the sovereign health-intelligence infrastructure of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia — a platform where every clinical, operational, and policy decision is informed by AI, and every AI decision is traceable, explainable, and accountable.

Sanad Technologies exists to ensure that as the Kingdom transforms its health system, the intelligence that drives it remains nationally owned, nationally governed, and structurally trustworthy.

---

## Principles

### 1. Sovereignty

Health data generated in the Kingdom belongs to the Kingdom and its citizens. The AI that reasons over that data must operate under Saudi jurisdiction, Saudi data-protection law (PDPL — نظام حماية البيانات الشخصية), and Saudi institutional oversight. SANAD Health is architected for national deployment under national control — not as a managed service whose terms, data residency, and algorithmic behavior are dictated by an external vendor.

### 2. Explainability

Every AI-generated decision in SANAD produces a structured explanation: the clinical factors that contributed to the conclusion, their individual weights, and the confidence level of each. A physician can read the reasoning. A regulator can audit it. A citizen can ask why. The system does not produce opaque scores — it produces accountable reasoning.

### 3. Consent

A citizen's data-sharing preferences are not advisory — they are enforced at the data boundary. SANAD's consent management covers data sharing, emergency access, insurance access, AI processing, research participation, and family health linking. Each consent type carries its legal basis under PDPL and MOH directives. Revoking consent has immediate, verifiable effect: the party that loses consent loses access in that moment, not after a review cycle.

### 4. Accountability

The Isnād audit architecture — a tamper-evident, SHA-256 hash-chained record of every decision, access event, and consent change — ensures that accountability is not a policy aspiration but a mathematical property. Any alteration to any record in the chain is detectable. The integrity of the entire chain is verifiable in a single operation.

## Where We Are

SANAD Health exists today as a fully functional platform with the following operational capabilities:

**Twelve role-based portals.** Physician, Citizen, Emergency, Ministry, AI Control, Laboratory, Pharmacy, Hospital, Insurance, Research, Family, and Supply Chain — each delivering role-appropriate intelligence and subject to role-appropriate access boundaries.

**A deterministic clinical decision engine.** Real-time risk scoring (0–100 composite from seven or more clinical signals), urgency classification (immediate / urgent / soon / routine) with SLA time windows, ranked contributing factors with confidence scores, and personalized clinical recommendations.

**A drug-interaction engine.** Severity-graded (critical / high / moderate / low) interaction detection across active medications and proposed prescriptions, operating at both prescribing and dispensing points.

**A digital-twin projection engine.** Twelve-month health trajectory modeling for individual patients, identifying intervention windows and projected outcomes under current trends.

**Tamper-evident Isnād audit.** SHA-256 hash-chained audit trail recording WHO, WHAT, WHEN, and WHY for every AI decision, access event, consent change, and clinical action. Integrity is verifiable.

**Consent enforcement at the data boundary.** Real-time enforcement of citizen preferences across all portals, with legal-basis documentation and instant effect.

**Fraud and anomaly detection.** Behavioral pattern analysis for insurance claims, distinguishing clinical severity from billing anomalies.

**Seven verified demonstration scenarios.** End-to-end scenarios using a coherent patient cast (the Al-Ghamdi family), each exercising a distinct engine capability, running against live engines and a real database — no mock data, no hardcoded outputs.

## Where This Goes: The 3–5 Year Horizon

### Phase 1 — Foundation (Current through Year 1)

Consolidation of the current platform, pilot deployment in select facilities, regulatory engagement with MOH and SDAIA, and formal PDPL compliance certification. Establish the operational baseline and institutional trust required for expansion.

### Phase 2 — Regional Expansion (Years 1–2)

Multi-facility deployment across a target region. Integration with existing hospital information systems and national registries. Establishment of the Isnād audit chain as the de facto standard for AI accountability in participating institutions. Expansion of the clinical decision engine to incorporate additional specialty modules.

### Phase 3 — National Fabric (Years 2–4)

Nationwide deployment across public and private sector health facilities. The platform transitions from a decision-support tool to a national health-intelligence fabric — the infrastructure layer through which population health analytics, disease surveillance, supply-chain optimization, and policy intelligence operate. Isnād becomes the Kingdom's auditable record of AI governance in healthcare.

### Phase 4 — Ecosystem and Export (Years 3–5)

Opening of the platform to third-party clinical AI models, each subject to the same Isnād audit and explainability requirements. SANAD becomes a trust layer — other AI systems can plug into the health-intelligence fabric, but every decision they produce is held to the same accountability standard. Exploration of the platform as an exportable sovereign-health-intelligence model for allied nations with similar data-sovereignty and governance priorities.

## What This Unlocks for the Kingdom

### Vision 2030 — Health Sector Transformation

The Kingdom's health-sector transformation requires more than new hospitals and insurance models. It requires a digital substrate that every participant — citizen, clinician, insurer, regulator — can trust. SANAD provides that substrate: interoperable, auditable, consent-governed, and AI-enhanced. The platform aligns directly with Vision 2030 objectives for healthcare quality, privatization enablement, and digital government.

### Localization of AI

SANAD represents a strategic investment in nationally controlled health AI. Rather than importing AI capabilities from foreign vendors — with the attendant dependencies on external data processing, foreign regulatory frameworks, and opaque algorithmic decision-making — the Kingdom builds and operates its own health-intelligence infrastructure. Clinical decision models are trained and validated against Saudi population health data. Arabic-language support is native, not aftermarket. Governance is Saudi governance.

### Citizen Empowerment

PDPL grants citizens rights over their data. SANAD operationalizes those rights in a way citizens can see and use — not as a legal document they sign, but as a set of controls they exercise. This transforms the citizen from a passive data subject to an active participant in their own health governance, and establishes a model for citizen-centric digital services across the public sector.

### Clinical AI Governance at Scale

As AI enters clinical workflows worldwide, every nation faces the question of how to govern it. SANAD's Isnād architecture offers a working answer: make every AI decision auditable by design, chain it cryptographically, and give regulators the tools to verify the chain. This positions the Kingdom as a leader in clinical AI governance — not by publishing guidelines, but by building the infrastructure that makes governance enforceable.

## Conclusion

SANAD Health is not a product roadmap. It is an infrastructure vision. The goal is not to build the best AI diagnostic tool, or the best electronic health record, or the best analytics dashboard. The goal is to build the trust architecture that makes all of those things possible — and accountable — at national scale.

The Kingdom has the ambition, the regulatory framework, and the institutional will. SANAD provides the architecture.
