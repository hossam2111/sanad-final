# 13 — Leadership Q&A / Objection Handling

Use this when leadership asks sharp questions. Keep answers short and anchored in the global product
identity.

## Is SANAD a Saudi product?

No. SANAD is a global sovereign AI health intelligence platform. The current demo uses a KSA
reference dataset because it is the completed proof environment. The platform supports deployment
profiles for different countries, but production rollout requires local integrations, hosting, and
regulatory sign-off.

## Is this already deployed for Qatar / UAE / another country?

No. What is ready today is the platform demo, the trust architecture, and the deployment-profile
model. A real country deployment would require integration with that country's health systems,
identity layer, hosting decision, legal review, and operating model.

## Does SANAD replace existing national systems?

No. SANAD sits above existing systems. It adds intelligence, governance, consent, and auditability.
The message is: "Use what the country already built; add the missing decision layer."

## Does AI make the final clinical decision?

No. SANAD supports the clinician. It explains risk, evidence, and recommended actions. The human
clinician remains accountable and confirms or overrides decisions.

## What makes SANAD defensible?

The moat is not one model. It is the combined layer of:

- Role-based workflows across 12 portals.
- Consent and ownership enforcement.
- Tamper-evident audit chain.
- Runtime AI provider control.
- Explainable clinical decisions.
- Deployment-profile configurability.
- Green verification gate and repeatable demo scenarios.

## What does the KSA reference dataset prove?

It proves the workflows and trust boundaries: doctor risk review, citizen consent, family access,
insurance gating, emergency retrieval, audit chain, AI governance, and national intelligence. It does
not prove another country's production hosting or regulatory status.

## What would a production deployment require?

1. Integration mapping with national systems.
2. Identity and patient-index integration.
3. Hosting and data-residency decision.
4. Regulatory/legal review.
5. Clinical governance and human-override policy.
6. Pilot site selection.
7. Monitoring, incident response, and audit operations.

## Why should leadership care now?

Health systems already have data. The missing layer is governed intelligence: who can access what,
what risk signals matter, what action should happen next, and how every sensitive action is audited.
SANAD packages that into one national operating layer.

## Safe Closing Answer

"We are not asking you to replace your national systems. SANAD is the intelligence and governance
layer above them. The KSA dataset is the reference proof. The platform is global, and the deployment
would be adapted to your country's systems, policies, and hosting requirements."
