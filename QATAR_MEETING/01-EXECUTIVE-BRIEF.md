# Qatar Executive Brief

## The Leadership Question

"What is different about SANAD for Qatar, what does it add, and do we already have something like it?"

## Short Answer

Qatar already has strong digital health foundations: HMC/PHCC electronic records, MyHealth patient
portal, Nar'aakom PHCC services, national health strategy direction, TASMU smart healthcare
ambitions, privacy law, and AI strategy direction.

SANAD should not be positioned as a replacement. The strongest position is:

> SANAD is the governed intelligence and decision layer above existing systems. It connects signals,
> explains risks, enforces consent and role boundaries, records audit evidence, and gives leadership
> sovereign control over AI use.

## What Qatar Already Has

| Area | Current Qatar position | Meeting implication |
|---|---|---|
| HMC/PHCC electronic health infrastructure | Digitized clinical workflows and EHR/CIS foundations | Do not sell SANAD as a basic EMR replacement |
| MyHealth | Patient access to parts of the medical record across HMC/PHCC | SANAD must be positioned beyond record viewing |
| Nar'aakom | PHCC mobile services such as appointments, dependents, health card workflows, and video consultations | SANAD should not compete with service booking |
| Qatar National Health Strategy 2024-2030 | Population health, patient care excellence, efficiency, resilience, integrated care, innovation | Map SANAD to measurable system outcomes |
| HMC Healthcare Strategy 2024-2030 | Integrated multi-provider care, digital technologies, precision medicine, patient-centered care | Lead with multi-provider intelligence and governance |
| TASMU Smart Qatar | Smart-sector healthcare direction and ecosystem stakeholders | Frame SANAD as a concrete operating layer for smart health |
| Privacy and AI direction | Personal data protection law and national AI strategy direction | Lead with consent, audit, human control, and sovereign AI |

## What SANAD Adds

SANAD adds five things that the existing stack is unlikely to provide as one governed operating
layer:

1. Cross-system decision intelligence: risk signals become explainable action.
2. Consent and role enforcement: patient/family/insurance/emergency access is governed and visible.
3. Audit evidence: access, AI recommendations, overrides, and decisions are traceable.
4. Sovereign AI control: provider/model/key configuration is controlled by the operator.
5. National command view: leadership can see risk, capacity, governance, and operational movement.

## What It Could Save Or Improve

Use directional value, not invented financial numbers:

- Fewer missed high-risk patients.
- Faster escalation and triage.
- Less manual chasing across systems and departments.
- Stronger citizen trust through visible consent and access control.
- Better governance evidence for AI, privacy, and clinical accountability.
- More value from existing digital health investments.

## What Not To Claim

- Do not claim Qatar has weak digital health infrastructure.
- Do not claim SANAD replaces Cerner/CIS, MyHealth, Nar'aakom, or TASMU.
- Do not claim exact cost savings without a local study.
- Do not claim a completed Qatar production deployment.
- Do not present Qatar as the product identity; SANAD remains a global sovereign health intelligence
  platform.
