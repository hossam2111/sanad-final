# 14 — Qatar Market Research Brief

Purpose: prepare the answer to the likely leadership question:

> "What is different about SANAD for Qatar, what does it add, and do we already have something like it?"

## Executive Answer

Qatar already has strong digital health foundations: HMC/PHCC electronic records, MyHealth patient
portal, Nar'aakom PHCC services, a national e-health/data strategy, TASMU smart healthcare ambitions,
Qatar National Health Strategy 2024-2030, and national AI/data-protection direction.

SANAD should not be positioned as a replacement for these assets. The right answer is:

> "Qatar has important systems of record and patient-service channels. SANAD is the missing
> intelligence, governance, and decision layer above them: it connects signals across systems, turns
> them into explainable clinical/operational actions, enforces consent and role boundaries, and gives
> leadership a sovereign AI control and audit layer."

## What Qatar Already Has

### 1. Electronic medical records and HMC/PHCC clinical infrastructure

Qatar has digitized major public-sector clinical workflows. HMC has used Oracle Cerner as its
Electronic Health Record / Clinical Information System. HMC also announced integration between
Oracle Cerner EHR and Qatar Biobank, enabling clinical/admin/lab data integration for research and
near-real-time data exchange.

Implication:
- Qatar does not need a basic EMR replacement.
- The opportunity is an intelligence/governance layer that reads from EMR/HIS/lab/payer systems and
  coordinates decisions.

### 2. MyHealth patient portal

MyHealth gives patients secure access to parts of their electronic health record, including lab
results, prescribed medications, appointments, diagnoses, immunizations, and visit summaries across
HMC and PHCC.

Implication:
- Qatar already has patient record access.
- SANAD adds consent orchestration, role-specific access enforcement, family/insurance/emergency
  decision workflows, AI explanations, and audit trails around access and actions.

### 3. Nar'aakom PHCC mobile services

Nar'aakom supports PHCC services such as health card workflows, appointments, dependents, changing
health center/family physician, and video consultations, with access tied to Qatar's Tawtheeq
national authentication.

Implication:
- Qatar already has citizen-facing PHCC service digitization.
- SANAD should complement it by adding cross-role intelligence and governed decision workflows, not
  by competing with appointment/health-card services.

### 4. Qatar National Health Strategy 2024-2030

The 2024-2030 strategy emphasizes population health, excellence in service delivery/patient care,
and health-system efficiency/resilience. Official launch material also mentions an integrated
healthcare system, clinical excellence, sustainability, innovation, and goals such as higher life
expectancy and reduced NCD mortality.

Implication:
- SANAD maps strongly to the strategy's efficiency/resilience, integrated care, and clinical
  excellence agenda.
- Lead with measurable system outcomes, not "new app" language.

### 5. HMC Healthcare Strategy 2024-2030

HMC's strategy calls for a more integrated multi-provider sector, better access and quality,
sustainability, efficiency, patient-centered care, emerging digital technologies, precision
medicine, and seamless affordable healthcare.

Implication:
- SANAD's strongest HMC-facing message is: "We make multi-provider care operational, explainable,
  and auditable."

### 6. TASMU Smart Qatar healthcare direction

TASMU identifies healthcare as a priority smart sector, aiming to improve access to quality
healthcare and mitigate chronic/systemic disease impacts, with KPIs including obesity, smoking, and
timely access to medical attention. TASMU also connects government, private sector, academia,
citizens, hospitals/clinics, insurers, pharmacies, and research stakeholders.

Implication:
- SANAD can be framed as a concrete smart-health operating layer that joins those stakeholders in
  role-specific workflows.

### 7. Data protection and AI direction

Qatar has a Personal Data Privacy Protection Law (Law No. 13 of 2016), and a national AI strategy
direction focused on local needs, strategic adoption, research, ethics, and national capability.

Implication:
- SANAD should emphasize sovereign AI control, consent, audit, human confirmation, and no autonomous
  clinical/insurance decisioning.

## Closest Existing Capabilities vs SANAD

| Existing Qatar capability | What it likely does well | What SANAD adds |
|---|---|---|
| Oracle Cerner / CIS / EMR | System of record; clinical documentation; orders/results; structured patient data | Cross-system intelligence layer, explainable risk, AI governance, role-specific decisions, national command view |
| MyHealth | Patient access to parts of record, labs, meds, appointments | Consent enforcement, family/insurance/emergency workflows, access/audit explanation, AI health plan |
| Nar'aakom | PHCC services, appointments, health cards, dependents, Tawtheeq access | Clinical decision intelligence and multi-role operating layer beyond service requests |
| TASMU smart healthcare | National smart-sector framework and ecosystem | A concrete health intelligence application layer that can plug into the ecosystem |
| Qatar AI strategy / research | AI direction, research and innovation | Operational AI governance: model/key control, explainability, audit chain, human override |
| Data privacy law | Legal baseline for personal data protection | Product enforcement: consent, role access, audit evidence, patient-visible trust |

## The Core Difference

Existing systems are mostly:

- Systems of record.
- Service access channels.
- Strategic frameworks.
- Individual AI/research initiatives.

SANAD is:

- A decision-intelligence layer.
- A trust/consent/audit layer.
- A cross-role workflow layer.
- A sovereign AI governance layer.
- A national command layer.

Short line:

> "Qatar has strong digital rails. SANAD is the intelligence train that runs across them, with brakes,
> audit, consent, and human control built in."

## What SANAD Would Save / Improve

Be careful: do not invent exact money savings without a local study. Use directional value.

### Clinical safety

- Fewer missed high-risk patients.
- Earlier drug-interaction detection.
- Faster escalation for urgent cases.
- Explainable recommendations reduce blind AI risk.

### Operational efficiency

- Less manual chasing across portals/systems.
- Faster triage and role-specific routing.
- Unified command view for ministry/hospital leadership.
- Better use of existing EMR/lab/payer data.

### Patient trust

- Consent becomes operational, not just legal text.
- Citizens can understand access and revocation.
- Family/insurance/emergency access becomes visible and governed.

### Governance and compliance

- Tamper-evident audit chain.
- AI provider/model/key controlled by leadership.
- Human override and reason capture.
- Evidence pack for sovereignty and data governance.

### Strategic value

- Supports Qatar's 2024-2030 health strategy priorities: integrated care, efficiency, resilience,
  innovation, and population health.
- Creates a platform for future AI use cases without letting every model become an isolated pilot.

## Likely Questions and Best Answers

### "Do we already have this?"

Answer:
"You have important pieces: EMR/CIS, MyHealth, Nar'aakom, TASMU, and national strategy. SANAD is not
trying to replace them. It sits above them as the governed intelligence and decision layer."

### "What is the main difference from MyHealth?"

Answer:
"MyHealth is primarily patient access to record information. SANAD is operational intelligence:
clinical risk, consent-gated workflows, emergency/family/insurance access, audit chain, AI governance,
and leadership command."

### "What is the main difference from Cerner/EHR?"

Answer:
"The EHR records and manages clinical data. SANAD reads signals from systems like the EHR and turns
them into explainable, role-specific action while enforcing consent, audit, and AI governance."

### "What will Qatar gain?"

Answer:
"Faster risk detection, safer medication decisions, operational command, citizen trust, policy-level
visibility, and a sovereign AI layer that leadership controls."

### "What would be required to deploy in Qatar?"

Answer:
"Integration mapping with HMC/PHCC and other providers, Tawtheeq/QID identity alignment, hosting and
data-residency decision, legal/privacy mapping to Qatar law, clinical governance, pilot sites, and
operational monitoring."

### "Is it production-ready for Qatar?"

Answer:
"The platform and reference demo are ready to show. A Qatar production deployment would be a defined
integration and governance project. We should not claim production deployment before integration,
hosting, regulatory, and clinical governance work."

### "What about AI risk?"

Answer:
"SANAD treats AI as assistive and governed. It keeps the human clinician/reviewer accountable, logs
decisions, explains evidence, and lets leadership control provider/model/key choices."

## Demo Ordering For Qatar Audience

1. Start with global product identity.
2. Acknowledge Qatar's digital maturity and existing systems.
3. Say SANAD complements, not replaces.
4. Show doctor critical patient.
5. Show consent/family/insurance trust boundary.
6. Show admin national command, audit, and AI Brain.
7. Show region profile as configurability only.
8. Close with deployment path: pilot, integrations, governance, evidence.

## One-Minute Arabic Answer

قطر لديها أساس رقمي صحي قوي: سجلات إلكترونية، بوابات للمريض، خدمات PHCC، وتوجه واضح في
الصحة الرقمية والذكاء الاصطناعي. سند لا يأتي ليستبدل هذه الأنظمة. سند يضيف الطبقة الناقصة فوقها:
طبقة ذكاء قرار، حوكمة، موافقات، تدقيق، وتحكم سيادي في الذكاء الاصطناعي.  

الفرق أن الأنظمة الحالية تحفظ البيانات وتقدم الخدمات، بينما سند يحوّل الإشارات المتفرقة إلى قرار
واضح ومفسر: من يحتاج تدخل؟ لماذا؟ من يحق له الوصول؟ هل توجد موافقة؟ هل تم توثيق القرار؟
ومن يتحكم في نموذج الذكاء الاصطناعي؟  

لذلك القيمة لقطر ليست "تطبيق جديد"، بل طبقة تشغيل وطنية تجعل الاستثمار الحالي في الصحة الرقمية
أكثر ذكاءً وأماناً وقابلية للقياس.

## Arabic Meeting Pack

### 30-Second Answer

سند لا ينافس ما لدى قطر. قطر لديها أنظمة سجل وخدمات رقمية قوية مثل HMC/PHCC وMyHealth
وNar'aakom. الفرق أن سند يعمل كطبقة ذكاء وحوكمة فوق هذه الأنظمة: يقرأ الإشارات، يفسر المخاطر،
يدير الموافقات والصلاحيات، يوثق القرار، ويعطي القيادة تحكماً سيادياً في استخدام الذكاء الاصطناعي.

### 90-Second Answer

لو سألونا "هل عندنا شيء مشابه؟" الإجابة المحترمة هي: لديكم أجزاء مهمة جداً، وهذا بالضبط سبب أن
سند مناسب لقطر. لديكم السجل الطبي، بوابات المريض، الخدمات الرقمية، وتوجه وطني واضح في الصحة
الذكية. سند لا يطلب هدم شيء أو استبدال منصة. سند يستفيد من هذه الأصول ويضيف فوقها طبقة
تشغيل: من المريض إلى الطبيب إلى الأسرة إلى التأمين إلى الوزارة.

القيمة ليست أن يرى المريض نتيجة مختبر فقط، بل أن يتحول النظام إلى قرار: من المريض عالي الخطورة؟
ما الدليل؟ من يحق له الوصول؟ هل توجد موافقة؟ هل القرار موثق؟ هل الطبيب يستطيع تجاوز توصية
الذكاء الاصطناعي مع سبب واضح؟ وهل تستطيع القيادة رؤية المخاطر والسعة والحوكمة على مستوى
وطني؟ هنا سند يضيف الفرق.

### Most Likely Pushbacks

| Pushback | Calm answer |
|---|---|
| "لدينا Cerner / CIS بالفعل." | ممتاز. هذا هو نظام السجل. سند ليس بديلاً عنه؛ سند طبقة قرار وحوكمة فوق السجل. |
| "لدينا MyHealth." | MyHealth يتيح الاطلاع على معلومات من السجل. سند يضيف الموافقات، تدفقات العمل، تفسير المخاطر، التدقيق، وتحكم الذكاء الاصطناعي. |
| "لدينا TASMU ومبادرات صحة ذكية." | سند يمكن أن يكون تطبيقاً عملياً داخل هذا الاتجاه: طبقة تشغيل تربط الأطراف وتحوّل المبادرة إلى نتائج قابلة للقياس. |
| "ما العائد؟" | تقليل التأخير، تقليل المخاطر المفقودة، رفع الثقة، توثيق القرارات، وتحويل الاستثمار الرقمي الحالي إلى قرارات تشغيلية. الأرقام الدقيقة تحتاج دراسة محلية. |
| "هل هو جاهز لقطر؟" | جاهز كمنصة وعرض مرجعي. الإنتاج في قطر يحتاج تكاملات، استضافة/إقامة بيانات، حوكمة سريرية، ومواءمة قانونية. |

### Do / Do Not

Do:
- ابدأ بالاعتراف بقوة البنية الرقمية القطرية.
- قل "طبقة فوق الأنظمة" لا "بديل للأنظمة".
- اربط سند باستراتيجية الصحة 2024-2030: تكامل، كفاءة، مرونة، ابتكار، صحة السكان.
- استخدم لغة سيادة وحوكمة: تحكم، موافقة، تدقيق، تفسير، مسؤولية بشرية.

Do not:
- لا تقل إن قطر لا تملك صحة رقمية.
- لا تقل إن سند سيستبدل Cerner أو MyHealth أو Nar'aakom.
- لا تعد بأرقام توفير مالية بدون دراسة محلية.
- لا تقل إن الإنتاج القطري مكتمل قبل التكاملات والحوكمة.

### Recommended Meeting Order

1. اعتراف: "قطر متقدمة رقمياً، ولذلك الحديث ليس عن تطبيق بديل."
2. الفرق: "سند طبقة قرار وحوكمة وتشغيل فوق الموجود."
3. الطبيب: اعرض اكتشاف المخاطر والتفسير.
4. المريض/الأسرة: اعرض الموافقة والصلاحيات.
5. الوزارة/الحكومة: اعرض القيادة الوطنية، التدقيق، والتحكم في AI.
6. المستثمر: اعرض قابلية التوسع كسوق سيادي عالمي، وليس تخصيصاً لقطر فقط.
7. الخاتمة: "Pilot محدود، تكاملات محددة، مؤشرات نجاح، ثم توسع."
